What's actually happening

underneath

You can name everything you just read. You've been told you're self-aware so many times the word has stopped meaning anything.

And you're still doing it. The override. The yes you don't mean. The performance of fine.

This isn't because you didn't get it. You got it.

It's because there is a woman underneath all of this who has not yet felt safe enough in her own body to relax. Until she does, she keeps reaching. For relief. For the next thing. For something outside of her that can give her the exhale she has not learned to give herself.

She doesn't need more information.

She needs to come home to her body, to herself. To check in on her. To nourish her. To claim her with compassion and love, exactly the way she is.

Not after she does one more thing to prove that maybe, now, she's worthy of love.

What becomes possible

  • You feel hunger and you stop eating when you're full.
  • You know when your yes is real and when it's a yes you're saying to keep the peace.
  • You cry when you're sad and you don't apologize in the middle of it.
  • You say a clean no. You receive a real yes.
  • You leave when you want to leave. You stay because you actually want to stay.
  • You stop texting the person who isn't texting back. Or you text them and feel grounded the entire time.
  • You feel your own desires before you feel everyone else's. You know what you want for dinner. You know what you want for your life.
  • You walk into rooms and your body settles instead of bracing.
  • You stop earning rest. You stop justifying joy. You stop proving you're enough.
  • You become a woman who is in love with her life. Not because everything in it is perfect, but because she's ok with knowing it's not.

And this isn't selfish

The thing your over-giving has been trying to do is take care of people. That's not going away. What changes is the from where.

Right now, you're trying to love people from a self that's running on empty. The love is real, and the resentment is also real, because you can't give from a self that has nothing left.

The people in your life don't need you tired. They need you home.

When you're available to yourself, you come home. And a woman who is home can be felt. The people you love finally get to experience all of you.

You stop being the martyr they secretly feel guilty around. You become the woman they get to enjoy.

This isn't turning away from your life. This is finally showing up to it.

And this isn't just about you. The women around you are watching. They are watching to see what they're allowed to want.

You being available to yourself doesn't just change your life. It changes what they believe is possible for theirs.
About Nina

Why I do this work

I spent years saying yes with my mouth and no with my body. I used substances to take the edge off being myself. I stayed in relationships that didn't see me, because being chosen, even badly, felt safer than being alone with me. I knew how to look like I was thriving while feeling almost nothing or way too much.

What changed wasn't another modality. What changed was that I built a relationship with myself that didn't break the moment things got hard.

"I became the one who claimed all of me."

I came home to my body intentionally, day after day. In this way the substances I depended on, and the dynamics that depended on me being smaller, began to fall away. I learned that my desires were not problems to suppress, but clues to who I truly am.

I'm an embodiment coach, breathwork and somatic practitioner, trained in parts work and in creating union between the masculine and feminine. I've worked with women individually and in groups, including in rehab settings.

But what actually qualifies me is this: I am in love with my own life, regardless of what is happening outside me. And I have the tools, and the trust, and the willingness, to take you there.

Questions you might
be asking

How is this different from everything else I've done?

Most of what you've done has worked on one layer at a time. The insight, or the practice, or the identity work. What we're doing here runs all of it together. The somatic work, the parts work, the identity work. At the same time, inside the same container. That's why things that were understood before finally get to land.

I'm already in therapy. Can I do both?

Yes, and I'd encourage it. This work isn't therapy and it doesn't replace it. It runs alongside good therapeutic support because we're working at a different layer: the body, the breath, the parts of you that talk therapy doesn't always reach. Women often find this work helps the therapy go deeper.

How much time do I need each week?

One 75-minute session per week, plus a daily 10-minute practice I record specifically for you. That practice isn't optional. It's the thread that carries the work between our sessions. Outside of that, this isn't homework-heavy. It's presence-heavy. You bring yourself. I'll take care of the rest.

What if I fall behind or miss a session?

Life happens. We'll handle it. Sessions are 1:1, so if something comes up we'll find another time. What I'll ask of you is honesty, with me and with yourself, about what's actually getting in the way. Sometimes what looks like a schedule conflict is the first thing we work on together.
